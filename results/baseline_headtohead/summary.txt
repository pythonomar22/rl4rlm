DETAILED BREAKDOWN
===============================================================================

1. NIAH (Needle in a Haystack) - 65.0%
   - By doc length: 5K=100%, 10K=60%, 20K=60%, 50K=40%
   - By position: 0.10=75%, 0.25=50%, 0.50=50%, 0.75=75%, 0.90=75%
   - Model finds needles reliably at short lengths, degrades at 20K+

2. Multi-NIAH (Multiple Needles) - 83.9%
   - By needle count: 3 needles=83%, 5 needles=78%, 8 needles=91%
   - By doc length: 10K=83%, 20K=55%, 50K=81%-95%, 100K=100%
   - Surprisingly strong at 100K with 8 needles

3. Doc Classify (Document Classification) - 88.4%
   - By doc count: 5=72%, 10=96%, 15=87%, 20=99%
   - Strong performance across sizes, weakest on small sets (5 docs)

4. Multi-Hop QA - 55.0%
   - Mixed results across 2-hop and 3-hop tasks
   - Many "Not found" failures on larger contexts (100K chars)

5. Code Debug - 28.9%
   - Found 50% of single-bug tasks, struggled with multi-bug
   - Often identified wrong function or wrong bug type

6. DataFrame QA - 73.3% (14 of 15 tasks completed, 1 timed out)
   - Strong on simple lookups and sector queries
   - Weaker on ranking and aggregation tasks
   - 50-ticker tasks (750K chars) cause context overflow crashes

7. Notebook QA - 80.0%
   - output_lookup and variable_trace: near-perfect
   - cross_cell: partial scores (0.5 often)
   - One variable_trace failure (got 10000 instead of 16677)

8. Hard NIAH - 100.0%
   - Perfect across all difficulty levels
   - Handled boundary, distractor, and extreme_length (up to 1M chars)
   - RLM scaffold enables reliable search even at extreme lengths

9. Verbatim Copy - 87.5%
   - 10K-20K documents: perfect reproduction
   - 50K: one failure (returned instructions instead of content)
   - 100K: one partial (prepended extra text)

10. Event Counting - 47.4%
    - Counting/aggregation tasks over long documents
    - per_entity counting: partial scores (undercounting common)
    - ratio/percentage tasks: close but often off by 1

11. Hard Multi-Hop - 10.0%
    - Only 1 of 10 tasks correct
    - Model cannot reliably decompose multi-hop questions
    - Frequent "Not found" or wrong entity returned
    - This is the benchmark where fine-tuning should help most

===============================================================================
KEY OBSERVATIONS
===============================================================================

1. STRENGTHS of base model:
   - Hard NIAH is PERFECT (100%) - the RLM scaffold enables search at any length
   - Doc Classify (88.4%) and Multi-NIAH (83.9%) are strong
   - Verbatim Copy (87.5%) works well for most document sizes
   - Notebook QA (80%) is solid

2. WEAKNESSES of base model:
   - Hard Multi-Hop (10%) - almost completely broken without training
   - Code Debug (28.9%) - struggles with multi-bug scenarios
   - Event Counting (47.4%) - counting/aggregation is imprecise
   - Multi-Hop QA (55%) - multi-step reasoning needs improvement
   - NIAH (65%) - surprisingly weaker than Hard NIAH due to different task design

3. OPPORTUNITIES for fine-tuning:
   - Hard Multi-Hop and Code Debug have the most room for improvement
   - Event Counting could benefit from better chunking strategies
   - NIAH degradation at longer contexts (50K) is addressable
   - Multi-Hop QA "Not found" failures suggest better search strategies needed

4. SURPRISES:
   - Hard NIAH (100%) vs NIAH (65%): Hard NIAH uses cleaner needle formats
   - Multi-NIAH at 100K (100%) vs NIAH at 50K (40%): task design matters
   - Base model already strong on Doc Classify and Notebook QA
